## 🛠 3. Summary of the work & Thinking Process

### a) Understanding the problem and the business objective
I defined the main goal of the project: identifying the unique and correct entity within the Veridion database for each raw client record. This Entity Resolution process is critical for providing the Procurement department with real visibility over aggregated supplier spend.

### b) Protecting primary data
To ensure traceability and prevent corrupting the initial data, I duplicated the `presales_data_sample` tab, working exclusively on a working copy inside the [Google Sheets Working File](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1wsrfq_P8K7mvhx5wPJueTVNUhPU98jUmsHzySGnoYqY/edit?usp=sharing).

### c) Visual validation
I initialized the process by filtering the `input_row_key` column. I identified and cleaned 2 Blank values. Subsequently, I isolated the group associated with key 0 (24-SEVEN MEDIA NETWORK) to understand the structure returned by the Veridion engine and to map out the primary matching logic.

### d) Quality checking the initial data
I ran integrity checks to identify the existence of possible duplicates at the dataset level before starting any manipulations.

### e) Implementing the first automated filtering rule (Exact Match)
I created the `match_status` (algorithmic calculation) and `analyst_notes` columns. I developed the first formula in the `match_status` column to evaluate a strict 2-level match (Geography + Name):
```excel
=IF(AND(LOWER(C2)=LOWER(P2), OR(LOWER(B2)=LOWER(M2), LOWER(B2)=LOWER(N2), LOWER(B2)=LOWER(O2))), "Exact match", "To verify")
```

The logic validates if the entered country and company name (input_main_country, input_company_name) and the country option generated by Veridion and one of the 3 name variants (full, legal, or commercial name) match 100%.

### f) Evaluating the first impact
Using a Pivot Table on the results of the new column, I automatically validated 48 records out of the total of 2951 rows as being perfect matches ("Exact match").

### g) Optimizing the algorithm (Partial Match)
To increase the automated success rate, I refined the formula in column K to accept partial matches (Fuzzy matching on substring). I applied the LEFT(..., 5) rule to match the first 5 characters of the names, always conditioned by a perfect geographical match.

Automated result: 48 Exact match, 970 Partial match, 1756 To verify. (The remaining records in the validated groups were classified logically).

```excel
=IF(AND(LOWER(D2)=LOWER(S2), OR(LOWER(B2)=LOWER(O2), LOWER(B2)=LOWER(P2), LOWER(B2)=LOWER(Q2))), "Exact match", IF(AND(LOWER(D2)=LOWER(S2), OR(LEFT(LOWER(B2), 5)=LEFT(LOWER(O2), 5), LEFT(LOWER(B2), 5)=LEFT(LOWER(P2), 5), LEFT(LOWER(B2), 5)=LEFT(LOWER(Q2), 5))), "Partial Match", "To verify"))

```

### h) Creating the Decision Cascade (Final Decision)
I introduced column L (final_decision) which acts as a centralized rules system (Decision Tree). This formula makes the final decision by checking the entire group and marking the "winning" records vs the "rejected" ones, based on a hierarchy: Exact > Partial.

New result: I decreased the number of manual records ("To verify") from 1756 to 557, generating correct exclusions for redundant records within the validated groups.

```excel
=IF(M2<>"", M2, IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $M:$M, "Match manual")>0, "Excluded (no match)", IF(K2="Exact match", "Match choosen", IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $K:$K, "Exact match")>0, "Excluded (have match)", IF(K2="Partial Match", "Match partial", IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $K:$K, "Partial Match")>0, "Excluded (have partial)", "To verify manually"))))))
```

### i) Managing rule collisions (Fail-Safe & Tie-Breaker)
Following the application of the automated rules, I observed cases where multiple candidates were validated with "Exact match" or "Partial Match" for the same input (collision). To maintain the 1-to-1 mapping business rule and avoid duplicates, I updated the logic in the final_decision column.

I introduced a validation condition (COUNTIFS > 1) that detects ties. If the algorithm finds multiple equal matches within the same group, it refuses the automatic decision and changes everyone's status to "To verify manually". This method protects data integrity, escalating ambiguities directly to the human operator.

```excel
=IF(M2<>"", M2, IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $M:$M, "Match manual")>0, "Excluded (no match)", IF(K2="Exact match", IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $K:$K, "Exact match")>1, "To verify manually", "Match choosen"), IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $K:$K, "Exact match")>0, "Excluded (have match)", IF(K2="Partial Match", IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $K:$K, "Partial Match")>1, "To verify manually", "Match partial"), IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A2, $K:$K, "Partial Match")>0, "Excluded (have partial)", "To verify manually"))))))
```

### j) Pivoting towards manual investigation (OSINT & Domain Verification)
For the remaining 557 records, I introduced the manual_override column. According to Veridion's documentation, the web domain is the primary identifier. Therefore, instead of extensive external manual research (Google), I optimized my workflow by visually checking (cross-referencing) the URLs and domains already returned by the Veridion API inside the dataset. This method massively increased the validation speed for exceptions.

```excel
=IF(M42<>"", M42, IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A42, $M:$M, "Match manual")>0, "Excluded (no match)", IF(K42="Exact match", "Match choosen", IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A42, $K:$K, "Exact match")>0, "Excluded (have match)", IF(K42="Partial match", "Partial choosen", IF(COUNTIFS($A:$A, $A42, $K:$K, "Match partial")>0, "Excluded (have partial)", "To verify manually"))))))
```
During the manual exception investigation phase, I noticed that the website_url and website_domain attributes provided by Veridion were often populated, even when the name match was weak. I used these domains for a quick visual verification (cross-referencing), validating the sites before resorting to a Google search from scratch.

### k) Securing human input (Data Validation)
To prevent typos during manual processing—errors that would have compromised the complex string-based formulas in the final_decision column—I locked column M (manual_override) using a dropdown list (strict Data Validation). This step transformed the file from a simple static report into a secured and error-resistant working environment.

###l) Analysis of geographical anomalies and noise in the data
During the QA phase, I investigated apparent discrepancies at the city level for groups classified as "Exact match" (e.g., client input "Zurich" vs. Veridion result "Bachenbulach" for the company CommScope). I concluded that the algorithm worked correctly, the difference coming from data granularity: the client's input often defines the region/canton, while the database returns the specific municipality.

I also identified completely irrelevant results in the raw set (e.g., the firm Whirlpool returned in the same group as CommScope), representing noise probably generated by registrations at shared addresses or law firms. The implemented decision cascade handled these anomalies optimally, automatically excluding the "garbage" and keeping the mapping on the correct entity, confirming the robustness of the formula in front of messy data.

### m) Transitioning to Python
I considered that I had reached the technical and viability limit of Excel in managing complex collisions. For this reason, using Python (the difflib library) and Artificial Intelligence as an execution partner, I developed a cascading scoring script directly on the original .csv file. By cleaning legal suffixes, fuzzy matching names, and applying geographical and digital tie-breakers, I managed to reduce the volume of manual work from 1,281 rows to just 138 rows to verify in the end.

### n) Integrating Python outputs
I used the same logic as in the Excel/Spreadsheet version, creating 2 columns: manual_override (with a droplist – Match manual and No Match) and analyst_notes. I modified column M (final_decision_python) from hardcoded to dynamic:

```excel
=IF(COUNTIFS($A$2:$A$3000, A42, $N$2:$N$3000, "Match manual")>0, IF(N42="Match manual", "Match choosen", "Excluded (have better match)"), IF(L42="Match choosen", IF(J42=K42, "Match choosen", "Excluded (have better match)"), "To verify manually"))
```

### o) Result of the Auditing and Manual Validation Process
To ensure maximum data accuracy, a granular manual analysis was performed on the remaining exception queue from the Python automation phase. The process included directly auditing the web domains provided by Veridion and cross-referencing via the Google search engine for ambiguous cases.

The final dataset, structured across 2,952 rows corresponding to a total number of 591 unique companies requested by the client, presents the following distribution:

564 companies (95.4%) were successfully mapped and associated with the reliable information generated by Veridion.

113 rows were explicitly labeled as No Match following manual validation, confirming that the requested geographical or legal entities are not found in the provided samples.

25 rows were isolated and proposed for secondary manual re-verification, to be unlocked following an alignment session with supervisors / project stakeholders.


## 📊 2. Data Analysis and QC Report

From the sample of **591 companies**, after finalizing the Entity Resolution process, I chose one option from those provided for **564 companies**, 18 companies needing a manual re-verification after a discussion with supervisors/stakeholders, and for 9 companies I did not have any suitable option. 

For the 564 companies with a chosen match, I performed an analysis of the data attributes to identify the consistency and completeness level of the final dataset. The objective is to deliver a *"Procurement Ready"* dataset that allows category managers to immediately start spend analysis and sustainability monitoring.

### A. Assessment of Consistency and Data Gaps
Following the SQL analysis of empty columns, I identified the following key insights:

* **Identification Integrity (Core Fields):**
  * We have **100% coverage (0% missing)** for critical attributes: `veridion_id`, `company_name`, `main_country`, `main_country_code`, and `last_updated_at`. This guarantees that each entry is unique, correctly located, and updated, eliminating the issue of *"messy"* or *"outdated"* data reported by the client.
* **Industrial Classification (Spend Analysis Support):**
  * **Strengths:** Primary classification codes (`naics_2022_primary_code`, `sic_codes`, `nace_rev2_codes`) have an availability rate of **85.28%** (only 14.72% missing). This is robust enough to support an automated segmentation of suppliers within the procurement department.
  * **Weaknesses:** Secondary codes (`naics_2022_secondary_codes`) are missing at a rate of **89.89%**. The inconsistency appears here because most suppliers in the sample are profiled strictly on their primary activity.
* **Digital Presence and Contact Channels:**
  * There is a discrepancy between web presence and contact info: although **79.26%** of companies have a website (`website_url`), only **64.36%** have a primary email (`primary_email`) identified.
  * Certain social media columns are irrelevant for this sample: `tiktok_url` has **100% missing data**, and mobile applications exceed **96% missing data**.

### B. Data Curation Strategy for the Client
To ensure a dataset ready for immediate deployment, I recommend the following data curation strategy:

* 🎯 **Standardization of Identifiers:** Using `veridion_id` as a unique anchor to prevent duplicates from reappearing in the client's database.
* 🧹 **Visual Optimization:** Removing columns with a missing rate above **90%** (e.g., TikTok, App URLs, Secondary NAICS) from the final deliverable to reduce visual noise and maintain focus on actionable data.
* 📋 **Name Consolidation:** Where `company_legal_names` is missing (**35.64%**), `company_name` (available 100%) will be used as a fallback, ensuring consistency in the client's reporting documents.
* 🌱 **Activating the Sustainability Pillar:** Although the client lacks resources for ESG research, Veridion attributes (`Sentiment`, `Pillar`, `Theme`) will be included as added value, allowing automated monitoring of sustainability risks without additional effort on their part.
